Characters

  • George
  • George is described as a small, quick-witted man.
  • He travels with and takes care of Lennie, however despite his often complaining about Lennie, seems devoted to his friend.
  • George is very protective of Lennie, which seems to be a driving force of his motivation.
  • He shares a dream with Lennie of one day owning and tending to his own farm.
  • Lennie
  • Lennie is an extremely large man who displays a childlike demeanor due to a slight mental disability.
  • He depends on his friend George for everything as without him he would struggle to survive on his own.
  • He holds onto an impossible dream of sharing a farm with George in the future.
  • Lennie has no idea his own strength, which coupled with his love for petting soft things, leads to a tragic end to the novel in which he accidentally kills a woman.
  • Candy
  • Candy is another ranch-hand that works with Lennie and George.
  • Candy is aging and struggles to keep up with the work as he is slowly becoming crippled and has already lost a hand to work.
  • He fears losing his job on the ranch and latches on to the dream that Lennie and George share of owning a farm.
  • Curly
  • Curly is the son of the ranch boss and does whatever he can to prove that he stands above the ranch-hands.
  • He is a very wicked man, often looking for confrontation and a reason to take his anger out on the other men.
  • He is recently married which only makes his situation worse as he is easily jealous and extremely suspicious of his flirtatious wife.
  • Curly's Wife
  • Curley's wife is the only female character mentioned, and is not given a name throughout the novel.
  • She is an attractive woman, who dreams of a better life much like the other ranch-hands.
  • Also like the other characters in the novel she is very lonely and searches for a way to improve her situation.
  • Crooks
  • Crooks is the black stable-hand, he is known as Crooks because he has a very crooked back from all the years of farm work.
  • Crooks is a very bitter man who often has a tough time getting close with any other men due to his disposition, as well as his skin color.
  • He does however seem to grow fond of Lennie, and the two spend time-sharing their dreams with one another.
  • Slim
  • Slim works on the ranch as a mule driver, he is of a much higher position than all of the ranch-hands, and is only below The Boss and Curley.
  • Slim is a very wise man and is a very comforting person due to his insightful words, he also is the only man in the novel who is satisfied with his place in the world.
  • The Boss
  • The Boss is in charge of the ranch and the father of Curley.
  • Though the Boss is only mentioned once, he seems to be a fair man as suggested in the text.
  • Carlson
  • Carlson is another ranch-hand; he often complains about various things, one in particular is Candy's old dog.
  • Carlson convinces Candy to put down the old dog, more so for his own incentives however.
  • Aunt Clara
  • She is never actually in the book except for when she is referred to by the narrator.
  • Aunt Clara took care of Lennie up until her death and was a very compassionate woman.
